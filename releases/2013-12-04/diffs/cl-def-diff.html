CL:0000860 : classical monocyte

OLD: A type of monocyte that responds rapidly to microbial stimuli by secreting cytokines and antimicrobial factors. This cell type is characterized by high expression of CCR2 in both rodents and humans, and of being larger size than non-classical monocytes.
NEW: A monocyte that responds rapidly to microbial stimuli by secreting cytokines and antimicrobial factors and which is characterized by high expression of CCR2 in both rodents and humans, negative for the lineage markers CD3, CD19, and CD20, and of larger size than non-classical monocytes.

CL:0000875 : non-classical monocyte

OLD: Subset of monocytes characterized by low expression of CCR2, low responsiveness to monocyte chemoattractant CCL2/MCP1, low phagocytic activity, but increased co-stimulatory activity. May also play a role in tissue repair.
NEW: A type of monocyte characterized by low expression of CCR2, low responsiveness to monocyte chemoattractant CCL2/MCP1, low phagocytic activity, and decrease size relative to classical monocytes, but increased co-stimulatory activity. May also play a role in tissue repair.

CL:0000939 : CD16-positive, CD56-dim natural killer cell

(was cytotoxic CD56-dim natural killer cell)

OLD: A mature natural killer cell that has the phenotype CD56-low and CD16-positive and is capable of cytotoxic function and cytokine production.
NEW: A mature natural killer cell that has the phenotype CD56-low, CD16-positive and which is capable of cytotoxicity and cytokine production.

CL:0002057 : CD14-positive, CD16-negative classical monocyte

OLD: A monocyte that is CD14-positive, CD16-negative, CD64-positive, CD163-positive,
NEW: A classical monocyte that is CD14-positive, CD16-negative, CD64-positive, CD163-positive.
