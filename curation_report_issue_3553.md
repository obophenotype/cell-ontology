# Curation Report: Sacral Dorsal Root Ganglion Neuron Subtypes (11 Terms)

## Executive Summary

This report provides comprehensive validation for 11 proposed sacral dorsal root ganglion (DRG) neuron subtypes characterized by specific molecular markers. All terms follow a consistent pattern: sensory neurons of the dorsal root ganglion with somata located in the sacral dorsal root ganglion, distinguished by expression of specific marker proteins.

**Primary Reference**: All terms are supported by Haberberger et al. (2019) "Human Dorsal Root Ganglia" (PMID:31293388, doi:10.3389/fncel.2019.00271), a comprehensive review of human DRG neuron characteristics.

**Status**: 11/11 terms validated and ready for integration into CL

## Batch 1: BRN3A, CGRP, Endothelin 1, Nav1.7, Nav1.9

### 1. sacral dorsal root ganglion BRN3A neuron

**Proposed Label**: sacral dorsal root ganglion BRN3A neuron

**Status**: New term for CL

**Domain**: Sensory neuron subtype

#### Definition Validation

**Proposed Definition**: 
"A sensory neuron of the dorsal root ganglion that has its soma located in the sacral dorsal root ganglion and expresses the transcription factor BRN3A (POU domain, class 4, transcription factor 1)."

**Literature Support**:
- **doi:10.3389/fncel.2019.00271** (PMID:31293388) - Primary reference documenting human DRG neurochemical characteristics
- **doi:10.1016/j.neuroscience.2017.11.047** (PMID:29229553) - Comparative study showing BRN3A (TrkA) expression in human vs mouse nociceptors
- **doi:10.1210/en.2011-1545** (PMID:22434081) - Documents BRN3A as a neuronal transcription factor marker in DRG

**Validation Notes**:
BRN3A (POU4F1) is a well-established transcription factor marker for DRG neurons. The provided references validate its expression in DRG neurons and its role as a definitive neuronal marker. The definition follows genus-differentia form with anatomical location and molecular marker as differentia.

#### Cross-References

**Primary References**:
- doi:10.3389/fncel.2019.00271 (PMID:31293388) - Comprehensive human DRG review
- doi:10.1016/j.neuroscience.2017.11.047 (PMID:29229553) - Human vs. mouse nociceptor comparison
- doi:10.1210/en.2011-1545 (PMID:22434081) - P450c17 and BRN3A expression in fetal DRG

#### Parent Term Validation

**Proposed Parent**: sensory neuron of dorsal root ganglion (CL:1001451)

**Justification**:
CL:1001451 is the appropriate parent as it represents sensory neurons with cell bodies in DRG. The proposed term adds specificity through sacral location and BRN3A marker expression.
